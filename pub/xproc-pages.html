What is XProc 3.0

XProc 3.0 is an information processing and pipelining stack based on (W3C Recommendation) XDM XQuery and XPath Data Model 3.1, a technology published and supported by its developer, customer and user community. In the form of a declarative language, it provides tools and their users a runtime architecture that is sustainable over time, scalable and adaptable.

As a standard supporting a common semantics across implementations — itself proof-tested by a history of earlier work — XProc 3.0 promises greater adaptability, accessibility, and scalability than prior solutions to the problem of pipeline orchestration and execution.

XProc 1.0 was published as a W3C Recommendation in 2010. In addition to integrating the latest XSLT and XQuery technologies such as XSLT 3.0, XProc 3.0 (finalized 2022) represents a significant advance over XProc 1.0, being

  • More streamlined and easier to learn and use
  • Open to any format or data notation, including JSON and plain-text-based formats (e.g. CSV, TSV etc.), in addition to XML
